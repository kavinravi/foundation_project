1. Purpose and Scope
This Investment Policy Statement governs the liquid investable assets of Whitmore Capital Partners, a
single-family office managing approximately $1.8 billion in diversified financial assets on behalf of the
Whitmore family. It establishes the portfolio's return objective, risk limits, approved asset classes,
benchmark definitions, and governance requirements.
The document anchors the 2026 strategic and tactical allocation framework in a consistent decision
process. The Investment Principal retains final authority over policy changes and exception approvals.
The Chief Investment Officer is responsible for implementation oversight and for escalating any actual or
projected breach of the hard constraints stated below.
2. Investment Objectives
The portfolio seeks a long-run total return of 8.0% annualized over rolling 5-year periods. The objective
reflects the family's preference for real wealth accumulation across generations.
The portfolio will be managed on a total-return basis. The family is open to non-traditional return sources
provided they are liquid, transparent, and accompanied by a clear economic rationale. The portfolio's
base currency is USD; all performance and risk metrics are measured in USD terms. Non-USD assets
carry embedded currency exposure unless explicitly hedged.
3. Risk Tolerance
The portfolio is expected to operate within a realized volatility ceiling of 15% annualized and a
maximum tolerated drawdown of 25% peak-to-trough. These constraints reflect the family's
willingness to accept meaningful short-term drawdowns in exchange for higher long-term compounding.
The family is comfortable with asset classes that exhibit high standalone volatility provided they improve
the portfolio's risk-adjusted return profile at the total portfolio level. Illiquidity is acceptable in moderation,
but all policy assets must be exchange-traded or redeemable within five business days.

6.2 Opportunistic Sleeve
The TAA overlay may temporarily allocate to assets outside the SAA universe for short-term hedging or
alpha capture. These Opportunistic positions are subject to:
 Maximum 5% of portfolio value per individual Opportunistic asset
 Maximum 15% of portfolio value in aggregate across all Opportunistic positions
 Positions must be closed or formally reviewed within 12 months of initiation

documentation within 48 hours.

AMENDMENT NOTICE (April 2026, Family Resolution 2026-02)
Following the Q1 2026 investment review, the Investment Principal resolved to revise one binding policy
limit effective immediately. The non-traditional allocation cap is increased from 15% to 20% of total
portfolio value, reflecting the family's updated conviction in the diversification benefits of non-traditional
stores of value during the current macro regime. All other constraints remain unchanged. The revised
threshold (20% non-traditional cap) supersedes the figure shown in the table above and should be used
as the binding constraint in all portfolio construction and optimization work performed under this IPS. The
table above retains the prior figure for audit trail purposes only.

A round-trip transaction cost of 5 basis points shall be applied to all portfolio turnover at each
rebalancing event, including both SAA rebalances and TAA-driven adjustments. Transaction costs must
be reflected in all performance reporting and backtest evaluations.
9. Rebalancing Policy
SAA weights will be rebalanced on the last trading day of each calendar year. TAA adjustments may
occur at any frequency when a documented signal triggers, subject to the governance requirements in
Section 6.3.
Emergency rebalancing may be initiated if any hard policy limit is breached or if the CIO determines that
market conditions require immediate action. Any interim rebalance requires written signoff from the CIO
and the Investment Principal and should normally be executed within two business days of approval.
10. Monitoring and Reporting
10.1 Scheduled Reporting
The CIO is responsible for producing the following reports on a fixed schedule. Both Benchmark 1 (60/40)
and Benchmark 2 (Diversified Policy Portfolio) serve as performance references in all reporting; neither is
a mirror of the strategic target allocation.
Monthly Risk Summary (delivered to the Investment Principal by the 5th business day of each month):
 Portfolio return (gross and net of transaction costs) for the prior month, quarter-to-date, year-to-date,
and inception-to-date, measured against both benchmarks
 Realized annualized volatility over trailing 1-month, 3-month, and 12-month windows
 Current drawdown from most recent peak, with date of peak
 Current allocation weights by sleeve and classification tier (Core, Satellite, Non-Traditional,
Opportunistic), flagging any weight within 2 percentage points of a policy limit
 TAA signal status (active or inactive) and any TAA-driven rebalances executed during the month
Quarterly Investment Review (presented to the Investment Principal within 15 business days of
quarter-end):
 All content from the monthly summary, plus rolling 5-year annualized return relative to the 8.0%
objective
 Return attribution decomposed by asset class and by SAA vs. TAA contribution
 Sharpe ratio (risk-free rate: prevailing 3-month US Treasury yield) measured against both
benchmarks
 Calmar ratio and Value-at-Risk (95%, historical) for the portfolio and both benchmarks
 Compliance confirmation that all hard constraints in Section 7 were satisfied throughout the quarter,
or documentation of any breach and remediation taken
 Forward-looking commentary on signal outlook and any proposed changes to TAA parameters
10.2 Event-Driven Reporting
A formal policy review is triggered immediately when any of the following thresholds is breached or
projected to be breached within five business days:
 Realized annualized volatility exceeds 15%
 Peak-to-trough drawdown breaches 25%
 Non-traditional allocation exceeds 20% (per Amendment 2026-02)
 Satellite allocation exceeds 45%

Upon a trigger event, the CIO must deliver a written incident report to the Investment Principal within 48
hours. The report must include the date and cause of the breach, the magnitude and duration of the
deviation, any corrective trades executed or proposed, and a plain-language explanation of the
remediation plan. The Investment Principal may require an extraordinary review meeting at their
discretion.
10.3 Annual Policy Review
A comprehensive IPS review will be conducted annually, coinciding with the year-end rebalance. The
review must evaluate whether the return objective, risk limits, benchmark composition, SAA targets, and
TAA framework remain appropriate given current market conditions, family circumstances, and observed
portfolio behavior over the prior year. Any proposed amendments require written approval from the
Investment Principal before implementation.
