Market stress:
Signals used would be HY OAS, VIX, SPX trend, and RTY/SPX relative performance. The idea is that when credit spreads widen, implied volatility rises, equity trends weaken, and small caps underperform large caps, financial conditions are deteriorating which increases downside risk. This is supposed to be as forward looking as possible. When stress worsens, the optimizer should reduce expected returns on US & Japan equities, China A-shares, REITs (high correlation to equities), Bitcoin and silver. It should shift towards US treasuries, TIPS, Gold, and Swiss franc. Some of the signals can be adjusted or dropped as necessary, especially the small cap large cap gap as i’m not sure if we’re allowed to use that (although I assume it’s fine considering it would just be a signal and not something we trade).
2.  Growth cycle:
Signals used would be ISM PMI and its 3 month change, Conference board confidence, UMich Sentiment, and RTY/SPX relative performance. The idea is that when business surveys and consumer sentiment improves, and small cap equities confirm that move, the economy is entering a more favorable growth regime. During this signal, we can take more exposure while staying within the IPS. The goal is to lean into growth rather than just reacting to increased risk, although this could breach the drawdown limits, so it is not my favorite. In strong growth regimes we can prioritize equities and REITs, with Bitcoin or silver as well (in small quantities). Move away from treasuries and Swiss franc.
3. Two-stage regime change:
Signals would be in two stages. The first one would be HY OAS, VIX, and SPX trend, with the second stage being ISM PMI and confidence or sentiment. The idea is that markets react and move first, then macro indicators confirm that move later. The TAA could use stage 1 as early detection of regime shifts, with the macro signals acting as confirmation, hopefully reducing false positives. If the market stress stage breaches a threshold, then we shift to risk off. When market stress improves and macro confirmation stops deteriorating, reenter the risk on regime. An alternative would be making the TAA shifts dependent on whether both layers agree (so less weight if one does and the other doesn’t, or full shift when both agree). Could be difficult to implement.
4. Stagflation Signal:
Signals include HY OAS, ISM PMI, VIX, TIPS vs Treasury performance, and gold trend. The idea is that when growth weakens but inflation protection assets hold up better, then the portfolio should shift towards inflation protection like TIPS and gold to fit the family’s objectives. If growth deteriorates and inflation hedges outperform then the TAA reduces equity and REITs exposure, preferring TIPS and gold over treasuries, and shift towards Swiss franc as well. If bitcoin is used (“digital gold” or whatever) keep it minimal to not blow up the volatility. This one could be hard to validate out of sample.
